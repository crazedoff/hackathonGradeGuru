Frequently Asked Questions

A GPA, or Grade Point Average, is a number that summarizes academic performance by averaging letter grades over a period of time.
The GPA Calculator consists of four different inputs.
You can add your class information to these inputs, and then click "Add Class".
The information for the class will go to the table below, and your weighted and unweighted GPA will start to show.
You can add as many classes, and marking periods, that you need.
If you wish to delete an individual marking period, you can do so, and the GPAs will change accordingly.
Once you are done calculating your GPA, you can press the "Delete All" button.
By utilizing these instructions, you can effectively calculate your weighted and unweighted GPA using the Bridgewater-Raritan High School's grading scale.
A weighted GPA accounts for the difficulty of classes by giving a numerical boost to more challenging courses, while an unweighted GPA treats all classes equally.
The letter grade "U" represents a numerical grade that is under a 65. It is quite similar to the letter grade "F".
The GPA calculations that Grade Guru uses are compatible with most institutions' grading systems.
However, the calculation system of Bridgewater-Raritan High School was specifically used.
